How service works

One clear path from problem to solution

Repair calls and replacement projects are different, but both begin with listening and diagnosis — not a sales presentation.

Schedule a Visit
  1. 01
    Before the visit

    Tell us what is happening

    Call or send the online form. Dispatch asks about the symptoms, equipment type, and timing, then confirms an arrival window.

    Appointment confirmation provided
  2. 02
    At your property

    We inspect before recommending

    The technician listens to your concerns, checks the system, and tests the components related to the problem. You receive an explanation of the cause, not just the symptom.

    Diagnosis explained in plain language
  3. 03
    Before work begins

    Review the options and price

    When more than one sensible solution exists, we explain the differences. The approved scope and price are documented before repair or installation starts.

    No work without approval
  4. 04
    The work

    Repair, install, and test

    We protect the work area, complete the approved scope, and run the system through a final operating and safety check before packing up.

    Workspace cleaned before departure
  5. 05
    After the visit

    Get the record and ongoing support

    Your service documentation covers the work completed and any next steps. If a question comes up later, our local office can pull the same record.

    Service history stays on file
What happens next

Repair or replacement?

The diagnosis determines the next step. We do not treat every older system as a replacement job.

If repair makes sense

Restore the system

We confirm parts availability, complete the approved repair, and verify the system in heating or cooling mode before leaving.

  • Common parts carried on service vehicles
  • Old component shown or documented
  • Repair warranty explained on the invoice
If replacement is the better value

Plan the right installation

We assess the home and existing ductwork, review equipment choices, and provide a complete proposal before scheduling installation.

  • Equipment sized for the property
  • Permit and removal details included
  • Controls and warranty reviewed at handoff
Before you book

Common questions

These are the details customers usually ask our dispatch team before the first visit.

How long is a typical service visit?

Most diagnostic visits take 60–90 minutes. The technician can give you a more accurate repair time after identifying the fault.

Will I receive a price before the repair?

Yes. We present the scope and price for approval before starting the repair. If the scope changes, we discuss that change first.

Do you work on equipment you did not install?

Yes. Our technicians service most major residential and light commercial brands, regardless of the original installer.

What should I do before the technician arrives?

Please clear access to the thermostat, indoor equipment, and outdoor unit. If possible, note any unusual sounds, smells, or error codes.

Ready to Book?

Pick a time online or call dispatch. Either way, you'll get a confirmation and a tech who knows your name before they knock.
